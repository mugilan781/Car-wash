Professional Tips

Crucial detailing habits to prevent paint damage and preserve vehicle value.

Always Wash in the Shade

Never wash or apply products in direct, hot sunlight. Solar heat dries soaps and water rapidly, leaving aggressive mineral deposits, etching water spots, and chemical streaks on your clear coat.

Use the Two-Bucket Method

Ensure you have a wash bucket and a rinse bucket equipped with grit guard filters. Wash mitts gather paint-swirling micro-contaminants; rinsing them in clean water first prevents scratch recurrence.

Avoid Household Dish Soap

Household detergents are formulated to strip grease and oil. Washing your car with dish soap strips protective waxes and paint sealants, while drying out rubber door seals and trim pieces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to popular questions about detailing maintenance and ceramic coatings.

How often should a car be professionally detailed?+
For optimal maintenance, we recommend a professional complete detail twice a year (spring and autumn). Regular maintenance washes should be performed every 1 to 2 weeks to safely wash away road grime, brake dust, and environmental hazards.
Does ceramic coating guarantee complete scratch resistance?+
No. While professional 9H coatings offer chemical protection, UV blockers, and superior hardness compared to standard clear coats, they are not scratch-proof. They will significantly resist light micro-marring, but key scratches or abrasive wash techniques can still scratch them.
What is the difference between polishing and waxing?+
Polishing is a corrective process using abrasive compounds to thin microscopic levels of clear coat to smooth out scratches and swirl marks. Waxing is a protective step that applies a thin coating layer (carnauba or synthetic) over the polished surface to add gloss and paint shelter.
Can I detail my car if it has PPF (Paint Protection Film)?+
Yes, but you must be careful. Avoid using heavy rotaries or direct heat near film edges. Ceramic coatings can be applied on top of PPF to provide additional self-cleaning properties and protection against staining and discoloration.
How do I maintain my ceramic coated car after detailing?+
Hand wash using a pH-neutral shampoo designed specifically for ceramic coatings. Avoid automatic tunnel washes with abrasive brushes, and use a silica-based booster spray every few months to sustain hydrophobic performance.
